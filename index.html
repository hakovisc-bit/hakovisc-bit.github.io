Hakob Mikayelyan

Art Historian & Artist

Hakob Mikayelyan is a professional art historian and artist. His path has been shaped by rigorous university education, the deep study of the philosophy of art, and apprenticeships under established masters of the craft. His work bridges the gap between meticulous historical research and monumental painting.

Throughout his career, he has collaborated with international cultural institutions, including projects with the Kościuszko Foundation. His academic and creative efforts are currently focused on large-scale historical reconstructions.

International Presence & Exhibitions: Geneva, Washington D.C., Paris, Moscow, Poland, and Australia.

The Era of Reason: A Philosophical Manifest

Studying the history of art reveals a chronicle of immense creation, yet one that is cyclically shattered by the chaos of war. My work is driven by the conviction that humanity must transcend its archaic patterns of destruction.

I advocate for the Era of Reason — a vision where the progress of civilization is safeguarded by logical governance and justice rather than force. Through art and historical reconstruction, we must confront the evils of the past to build a future defined by intellectual evolution and the preservation of life.
